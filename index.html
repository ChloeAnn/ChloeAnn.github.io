Who Am I

Ethical AI is possible and necessary and I intend to see that it materialises.

Hi I'm Chloe.
I was born and raised in Newport, South Wales.
Ydw, dwi'n siarad Cymraeg ychydig ac rydw i'n ymarfer yn gyson.

I am passionate about mathematics and more importantly demystifying mathematics for young people. I have collaborated with some amazing organisations that work tirelessly to to encourage STEM learning in the black community.

My love for mathematics and modelling the world around us led me to speech technologies 7 years ago. My PhD focused on Speech Recognition, my first three years in industry I worked on Speech Synthesis systems and I have now found myself at home working on Knowledge Graphs and NLP techniques.

I want nothing more than a quiet, peaceful life as a researcher, however this isn't an option for me while the research community is exclusive, therefore I advocate for a more diverse, gender balanced community and fiercly believe that educational diversity is the key to innovation.

Research Experience

Apple Machine Learning Engineer

  • Project workflow consists of exploring recent literature, prototyping and evaluating models iteratively.
  • Neural TTS style transfer techniques for a prosody transfer task using - direct prosody transfer, Global Style Tokens, VAE, GMVAE, PT-GST have been explored.
  • Multi-modal applications looking at audio-visual synthesis combining computer-vision and neural TTS techniques.
  • Emotional/Expressive TTS in particular architectures that can interpolate between emotion categories.

Ph.D Automatic Speech Recognition

  • Studied a number of statistical speech recognition models including methods used in industry involving neural networks.
  • My work focused on adapting a speech recognition system that is a more faithful, parsimonious model of speech production. I developed and implemented a novel acoustic model of speech using C++.
  • Debugging and optimising my software, reducing run-time by at least a factor of 8 by considering different data structures.
  • Collaborated on classification experiments using linear algebra and applied statistics to examine sound dependent discriminative information.

Apple Internship with Text-To-Speech Team

  • Prosody transfer methods for tts systems.
  • Researched techniques that can be adopted to extract prosodic information from speech generating a phrase dependent prosody specification.
  • Developed a flask webapp and implemented researched statistical techniques that transfer a target prosody from a specification to a synthesised utterance.

MSc Mathematics of Complex Systems

Dissertation title: The study of an inclined internally heated fluid flow.
  • Designed and studied a theoretical model of an internally heated fluid flow with varying conditions.
  • Numerical solutions were implemented following an analysis of non-linearity of the Navier-Stokes Equation with varying initial conditions.
  • Study of Chaos theory and computation of bifurcations for Navier-Stokes

BSc Mathematics

Final year project: Belief propagation within an electricity network
  • Literature review on Bayesian networks and probabilistic modelling ‘Message Passing’ algorithms.
  • Developed and studied a toy electricity network using belief propagation.
